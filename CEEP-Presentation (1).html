Our Mission

"Powering the Future, Sustaining the Planet."

CEEP (Clean Environmental Energy Project) is dedicated to transforming the global waste crisis into a sustainable energy solution.

Our mission is to innovate and implement advanced recycling technologies that convert waste into valuable energy resources, including crude oil and jet fuel, while simultaneously reducing landfill waste and carbon emissions.

02
About Us

At CEEP, we are committed to:

Recycling & Recovery

We process over 100,000 tons of waste annually to produce 525,000 barrels of crude oil and 260,000 barrels of jet fuel, utilizing advanced technology for efficient conversion.

Sustainable By-Products

Our operations generate valuable by-products like black char and methane gas, which are then repurposed into advanced material essential for sustainable industries.

Environmental Stewardship

We believe in leading the charge towards a cleaner, greener future by reducing reliance on fossil fuels, minimizing landfill contributions, and lowering carbon footprints across various sectors.

Community Engagement

We at CEEP, foster awareness and collaboration within the communities to support various sustainable practices and encourage their participation in our initiatives.

Vision for the Future

We envision a world where waste is transformed into a powerful resource that meets our energy needs while safeguarding the planet for future generations.

Our Feedstock

From Waste to Wealth

We source abundant waste materials that would otherwise burden landfills and the environment, transforming them into valuable energy resources.

Waste Tires

Over 300 million scrap tires are generated annually in the U.S. alone. Our advanced pyrolysis technology breaks down tire rubber into valuable hydrocarbons, recovering up to 40% as oil and 35% as carbon black.

The U.S. faces significant challenges in waste management and energy production:

Waste Accumulation

Increasing volumes of construction debris, tires, and other refuse contribute to landfill overflow, leading to environmental degradation.

Energy Demand

The alternative energy market, valued at $943.39 billion in 2023, is expected to reach nearly $2,066.08 billion by 2030, indicating a growing need for sustainable energy sources.

Rising Jet Fuel & Crude Oil Demand

Global jet fuel demand is rising, with consumption reaching approximately 1.65 million barrels per day in the U.S. in 2023 and projected to hit about 8 million b/d globally by end of 2024.
